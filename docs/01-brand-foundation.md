# Kochie Engineering — Brand Foundation

*A master brand for Robert Koch's personal engineering practice and the things it ships.*

---

## 1. The one-line story

**Kochie Engineering is a Melbourne-based engineering studio of one — Rob Koch — building software for people who like software done well.**

Everything under the brand is downstream of that sentence. The blog is what Rob *thinks*. The products are what he *ships*. me.kochie.io is *who he is*. kochie.io is the *front door* that ties them together.

## 2. Brand architecture

A **master brand + sub-brand** system. The master is Kochie Engineering. Each property is a node with a clear role:

```
                    Kochie Engineering         (kochie.io)
                            │
        ┌───────────────────┼───────────────────┐
        │                   │                   │
     Persona              Writing             Products
   (me.kochie.io)     (blog.kochie.io)    (touch-typer, MetaFixer, …)
   "Rob"              "Kochie Engineering   each gets its own
                       Blog"                 product identity, but
                                             clearly part of the family
```

**Naming convention.** Sub-properties use a slash-style relationship in copy and lockups: `Kochie Engineering / Blog`, `Kochie Engineering / Touch Typer`. In URLs they remain as `*.kochie.io` subdomains, which is consistent with what you have.

**The split that matters.** Rob (the human) and Kochie Engineering (the studio) are different speakers. Rob writes me.kochie.io in the first person. The blog and product sites speak as "Kochie Engineering" — slightly more institutional, but never corporate. Think *Linear's voice when Karri writes vs. when the product speaks*.

## 3. Positioning

**For** working developers and the engineering-curious  
**Who** want technical content that's actually substantive and software that respects them  
**Kochie Engineering is** a one-person engineering studio  
**That** ships small, sharp products and writes about the messy parts of building them  
**Unlike** the agency-shaped marketing layer most independent engineers project, or the generic dev-influencer brand,  
**Kochie Engineering** stays specific, technical, and a little dry — the way good engineers actually talk to each other.

## 4. Audience

**Primary: Fellow developers and engineering peers.**

This is the audience you confirmed. Practically, it splits into three overlapping circles:

- **Working engineers** searching for real answers (the lambda-recursion crowd, the S3-bucket-limit crowd). They arrive via blog posts and Google.
- **Indie / open-source-curious devs** who try Touch Typer, find their way to the blog, then to Rob.
- **Hiring managers, conference organisers, podcast hosts** who land on me.kochie.io. They're not the *primary* audience but the brand has to read credibly to them — and the dev-peer voice does that work for free.

**Who Kochie Engineering is *not* for.** Non-technical SMB owners shopping for a "web design agency". The current kochie.io copy ("global support, multilingual team, 24/7") is fishing for that audience and it's the wrong audience. Cut it. (More in `03-per-site-positioning.md`.)

## 5. Voice and tone

**Voice** is the constant — how Kochie Engineering always sounds.  
**Tone** flexes by context.

### Voice attributes (with examples already on the sites)

- **Dry and self-aware.** *"I build things, write the code for them, and run from the resulting explosion."* / *"Find out when I sporadically scream into the void..."*
- **Technically substantive, never performative.** A 52-minute read on contact tracing with AWS CDK is on-brand; a 280-character hot take on AI is not.
- **Direct, slightly grumpy when warranted.** *"I'm not sure why this still needs repeating, but please—stop writing recursive Lambdas."* That energy is a feature.
- **Curious across domains.** Halo physics, city flag design, S3 internals, Australian banking — the throughline is "engineer poking at the world." Lean into that breadth, don't apologise for it.

### Tone register by surface

| Surface | Register | Example move |
|---|---|---|
| me.kochie.io | First-person, warm, slightly self-deprecating | "I'm Rob." |
| blog.kochie.io | First-person, opinionated, technical | "Stop writing recursive Lambdas." |
| Product sites (Touch Typer) | Second-person, helpful, plain | "Learn at your own pace." |
| kochie.io | Third-person but human, confident | "A studio of one. Melbourne." |
| Newsletter / micro-copy | Knowing winks allowed | "scream into the void" |

### Words to use
*ship, build, dig in, the messy parts, real numbers, opinionated, why this matters, here's what broke*

### Words to retire
*solutions, leverage, empower, journey, ecosystem, world-class, robust, seamless, 24/7 multilingual support*

## 6. Mission, vision, values

These are short on purpose. Engineers smell padding immediately.

**Mission** — Make small, sharp software and write honestly about how it gets made.

**Vision** — A Melbourne studio that other engineers trust as a source of substantive work and substantive writing.

**Values**

1. **Specific over generic.** A real number beats a vibe. A working repo beats a thread.
2. **Ship before you sermon.** The blog earns its voice because the products exist.
3. **Respect the reader's time.** Either go deep enough to be useful or stay short enough to be quotable. Nothing in between.
4. **Show the work.** Code, screenshots, slides, talks — open by default.

## 7. Brand promise

> If Kochie Engineering put their name on it, an engineer can trust it.

That's the bar for every product page, every blog post, every README, every conference slide. If it doesn't clear that bar, it doesn't ship under the brand.

## 8. Naming + nomenclature

- **Studio name (formal):** Kochie Engineering
- **Studio name (informal, dev contexts):** kochie.io
- **Person:** Rob (everywhere). "Robert Koch" only in formal/CV contexts.
- **Product naming pattern:** Plain, descriptive, two words max where possible. *Touch Typer*, *MetaFixer* both fit. Avoid cute portmanteaus.
- **Sub-brand lockup in copy:** `Kochie Engineering / Touch Typer` or `Touch Typer, by Kochie Engineering` — never just floating with no parent acknowledgement.
- **Email convention:**
  - `robert@kochie.io` → personal correspondence
  - `hello@kochie.io` → studio / product / general
  - `rob@kochie.io` → you may want to consolidate to this; pick one and stick

## 9. What's in / what's out

**In** — engineering, software, hardware, electronics, performance, security, AWS internals, indie products, talks, open source, running, bouldering, gaming (when it dovetails into physics or tech), Melbourne tech community.

**Out** — generic agency framings, fake team voice, AI hot-takes without a working artefact behind them, lifestyle content disconnected from engineering, anything that requires inventing a "we" that isn't real.

## 10. Decisions deferred
